The best dictation apps for Mac in 2026

By the Lazytype teamJune 14, 20266 min read
Short answer

For most Mac users, Superwhisper is the best all-round pick: local, private, works in any app, one-time option. MacWhisper is best for transcribing audio files, Apple Dictation is the free built-in option, and Wispr Flow is the slickest cross-device subscription.

macOS is spoiled for choice on dictation. Here are the apps worth your time in 2026, what each is best at, and an honest note on where Lazytype fits.

1. Superwhisper — best all-round

Runs Whisper locally on your Mac, types into any app, and offers both one-time and subscription pricing. Private, fast and well-designed. For most people asking this question, it is the answer.

2. MacWhisper — best for files

If your main job is turning recordings, meetings or interviews into text, MacWhisper's file-based workflow and editor are excellent. It also does live dictation, but files are its strength.

3. Apple Dictation — best free option

Built into macOS. Turn it on in System Settings and press the dictation key. Accuracy and features trail the Whisper-based apps, but it costs nothing and works offline once the language is downloaded.

4. Wispr Flow — best cross-device

If you want the same polished dictation on Mac, iPhone and Windows, Wispr Flow delivers, at a ~$15/month subscription. Cloud-only. See our Wispr Flow comparison.
